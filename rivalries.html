Arsenal vs. Manchester United

Arsenal vs. Manchester United is a truly legendary rivalry. I cover the warring captains below, but this is a fixtures that's bound to get any fan of football, no matter what team you support, to actually tune in. This rivalry is actually one that the other team has gotten the better of us. Manchester United handed Arsenal is heaviest ever defeat in the Premier League by slapping us up 8-2. That defeat actually led to some notable panic buys of players like our future team Captain and future Club Manager Mikel Arteta. Some good things can actually come out of heavy defeat!

The North London Derby

Arsenal vs. Tottenham, also known as the North London Derby is the biggest derby game in English football. The rivalry between these two teams simply stem from the fact that both teams are in North London. Personally speaking, I don't even really consider this an actual rivalry. Tottenham are never really a big threat to Arsenal and I expect to win every match against them. They're not a big club. They're not title challengers like we are, and their biggest accomplishment of any season would be to beat Arsenal simply because we know they won't win any trophies. Of this "rivalry" Arsenal have won the most games with 83 wins, Spurs with 61, and 52 draws.

A Tottenham fan's most horrible memory will be the time that Arsene Wenger stole their team Captain, Sol Campbell. Even to this day, over 20 years after he left Spurs, fans still consider him a Judas! That's how badly that transfer hurt them. Campbell said he had to leave Tottenham to win a trophy. Sol Campbell leaving Tottenham to Arsenal is still considered to be the most controversial transfer in the history of the league, and I fully agree with that sentiment. Patrick Vieira vs. Roy Keane

It should come as no surprise to any Arsenal or Manchester United fans that the rivalry between Arsenal Captain Patrick Vieira and Manchester United Captain Roy Keane is worthy of its own headline. Both players were ultra-competitive. They took winning, and losing, personally. They played the game at such an intense level, that there's absolutely no way that style would be acceptable in the league today. From these two players having arguments and squabbles between them in the tunnels before even stepping onto the grass, to giving each other clear reckless fouls without a single attempt to play the ball, these two players embodied what it meant to play for the badge on your shirt above all else. Vieira vs. Keane is probably the most famous player to player rivalry in the history of the Premier League.
