Arsenal's previous home stadium, the legendary Highbury was old and didn't seat enough fans to move Arsenal into bigger status than it was. It was due time for a revamp for the club's facilities. In 2007, Arsenal moved into it's 60,000 seater stadium, Emirates Stadium which was sponsored by the Emirates Airline.

With a new stadium, the club had to spend more money to pay for the new stadium. Unlike in the US where sports venues are paid with taxpayer funds, Arsenal had to spend much of it's own money to pay off the loans from the stadium's construction. Unfortunately, the club had to sell off its best players and go years without buying new stars to keep up with the new style of team-building that we were used to.
Fans were crushed to see the likes of Thierry Henry, Patrick Vieira, Cesc Fabregas, and Ashley Cole go off to new teams and win trophy after trophy, while the club went through a period of 9 years without winning a single trophy. The club became a meme for opposition supporters, especially since we replaced our winning champions with weaker, cheaper, and less-talented players.

It wasn't until a horrid 8-2 defeat to Manchester United did the club start to reconsider its style of recruitment and spending for players. Arsene Wenger brought in fresh new talent like Santi Cazorla, Mikel Arteta (who would go on to manage Arsenal), Mesut Ozil, Olivier Giroud, Alexis Sanchez, and Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang (which was Arsene Wenger's final Arsenal signing before he retired).
The club began to get back to winning ways and won its first trophy in 9 year with an FA Cup win over Hull City in 2014.
